West Pokot Woman Who Wedded Holy Spirit Refuses to Share Bedroom with Husband

Elizabeth Nalem, a mother of six from Makutano Town, said God appeared to her when she was asleep and requested her to leave her husband.

On Tuesday, June 1, the 41-year-old returned to her matrimonial home accompanied by her bridesmaid.

She, however, refused to sleep at home after the man asked the bridesmaid to leave.

“I will not go with this man. I am married to the Holy Spirit. I don’t want him. I was told to come back home. I don’t want any hindrance on my way. I want my rights as I continue with my new job,” Nalem said as quoted by Nation.

Her husband, Joshua Nalem, who is still puzzled by his wife's behaviour, said he had not quarrelled with her that could have forced her to change her mind about their marriage.

He added that he had paid her bride price of 22 cows and 15 goats.

Arrested

After her wedding with the Holy Spirit, she was barred from entering Uganda, where she was going for her honeymoon.

The lady was intercepted by the officers at Makutano town and taken to Makutano Police Post.

However, she was later released on Tuesday, June 1, upon her husband’s request.

West Pokot county police Commander Jackson Tumwet said Nalem was counselled while in custody, adding that she might be having a psychiatric problem.

“We talked with her while she was in our custody and suspected she might be having a psychiatric problem; that is why she has been doing all these,” said Tumwet.

Tumwet said Nalem’s husband had filed a complaint that his wife neglected her six children while committing to her newly found love.

He added that the woman had already plan go to Tanzania, Ethiopia and Israel upon returning to Kenya from Uganda.
