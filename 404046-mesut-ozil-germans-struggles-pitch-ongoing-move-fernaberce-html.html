Mesut Ozil: German's struggles on the pitch still ongoing despite move to Fenerbahce

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Saturday, June 29, 2024

- Ozil is struggling to make an impact at his new club Fernaberce

- In 322 minutes of play time, the German is yet to register a goal or assist for his new club

- Fernaberce are currently third on the log in the Turkish Super Lig

Mesut Ozil has been struggling to hit top form since his famous move to Fernaberce if stats are anything to go by.

As reported by TUKO.co.ke, Arsenal terminated Ozil’s hefty contract and the German made a move to a club he admired as a child, partly to fulfil his mother’s wishes.

The attacking midfielder was handed the number 67 shirt which symbolised his childhood roots and eventually made his debut on February 2 against Hatayspor.

Fernaberce won that game 2-1 thanks to a late winner with Ozil involved in the build up to his side’s winner but since then, his club has experienced a dip in form and the 32-year old is struggling to stamp his mark in the Turkish Lig 1.

In the five games he has been involved in for Fernaberce, the team has lost three times.

They were knocked out of the Turkish Cup by bitter rivals Istanbul Basaksehir and their title chances are slipping away after a run of unwanted results.

The most recent result saw them succumb to a shock home defeat against mid-table side Goztepe and they now find themselves in third place on the log, three points behind leaders Galatasaray.

While it has not been a disastrous start to his Fernaberce career by any stretch, the fact that Ozil is yet to record a goal or assist in his first five appearances for the club should ring some alarm bells.

During his days as a Real Madrid and Arsenal player, Ozil was at the peak of his powers, assisting goals for fun.

His performances on the pitch are not the only thing Ozil has come under scrutiny for.

German media have particularly been critical of the German for passionately singing the Turkish national anthem before games.
