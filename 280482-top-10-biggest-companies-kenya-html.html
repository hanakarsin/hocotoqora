Top 10 biggest companies in Kenya

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Thursday, May 9, 2024

The financial resources of the country have grown immensely in the past few years, and this is one of the reasons as to why Kenya is among the largest economies in East Africa by GDP. Due to this, numerous companies have established themselves in Kenya to exploit the available opportunities in the huge market. Some of these are owned by investors who are citizens of the country, while others are foreign investments. Generally, the region forms a great business location, and many companies have earned profits that enable them to grow to world class level. So, which are the biggest companies in Kenya?

The list of international companies in Kenya has greatly improved the country’s business outlook, and most of these companies have an impressive performance in terms of amazing service delivery to their clients. They are also known to pay their employees handsomely and they top the list of company's Kenyans seek to work with. Kenyan companies directory contains all the multinational corporations in Kenya and a list of local companies in Kenya.

Which are the biggest companies in the country?

The biggest companies in Kenya are engaged in different sectors of the economy such as communication, financial services, manufacturing, transportation, farming among other economic activities. The list of Kenyan companies by sector is basically classified into 5 categories which include private, international, registered, new companies and multinationals. All these firms have greatly contributed to the country’s economic growth and have also created job opportunities in Kenya. The 10 biggest companies in Kenya are discussed below.

1. Safaricom

Safaricom Limited Company is the leading mobile network operator in Kenya. It started in 1993 as a department of the former state-owned Telecommunications Company, Kenya Post, and Telecommunication Corporation.

Bob Collymore is the company's current CEO after succeeding Michael Joseph who led the company for close to 10 years. The headquarter is located in Westlands, Nairobi, Kenya. It has other branches in major urban centres of Kenya which include Mombasa, Eldoret, Nakuru and Kisumu.

The company has employed over 5,000 people with the majority based in Nairobi. It has also invested in the nationwide dealership and this has ensured that their clients have access to their products and services. The estimated Safaricom subscriber base is approximately 29.5 million.

2. East Africa Breweries Limited (EABL)

East African Breweries Limited is popularly referred to as EABL. The company was founded in 1922 as Kenyan Breweries Limited. It later merged with Tanganyika Breweries Limited and together they formed the now East African Breweries Limited. Over time, the company has continued to grow and expand its market territories and has even opened other breweries such as Mombasa breweries. The company operates in Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda and South Sudan.

The company has become successful in its manufacturing of beer, spirits, and adult non-alcoholic beverages. Tusker is the main brand of East African Breweries Limited and holds over 30% of Kenyan beer market. The other brands include Pilsner, Tusker Lite, Malt Lager, Guinness, Serengeti Premium Lager, White Cap Lager, Allsopps Lager, Senator Lager, Balozi Lager, Bell Lager, Chrome Vodka, Smirnoff, and Kenya Cane.

3. Equity Bank

Equity Bank is a financial service provider in Kenya. Its head office is located in Nairobi, Kenya. The Central Bank of Kenya has licensed the bank as a commercial bank. Equity bank maintains 173 branches and 38 of them are based in Nairobi.

Equity Bank, which has attracted recognition locally and internationally, is known to offer customer focus financial services and this has empowered their clients both socially and economically. The bank is also privileged to have been invited on many international forums to share its success.

4. Kenya Commercial Bank

Kenya Commercial Bank commonly known as KCB is a financial service provider and its headquarters are in Nairobi. The bank is registered by the Central Bank of Kenya as a commercial bank.

The bank has a total of over 250 branches all over Kenya serving more than 15.7 million account holders across the region. This makes it the largest banking network in Kenya, for now. KCB is known for giving out loans, offering mortgages services and saving funds for individuals and corporates.

5. Co-operative Bank of Kenya

Co-operative Bank of Kenya is also another financial service provider based in Kenya. It is licensed as a commercial bank by the Central Bank of Kenya. The institution has a strong presence throughout the country. From 1965, the company was registered under the Co-operative Societies Act.

Coop bank became functional in 2008 after it was resolved that it should be incorporated in the country’s Company Act. The financial institution runs three subsidiaries and they include, Kingdom Securities Limited, Co-op Trust Investment Services Limited and Co-op Consultancy and Insurance Agency Limited.

6. Kenya Airways Limited

The country’s airline was established in the year 1977, a decision that was implemented after the dissolution of the east African airways. The company is both privately and publicly owned, and the Government of Kenya is the largest shareholder.

The airline is usually regarded as the 'Pride of Africa' since it connects the continent to the world through its hub Jomo Kenyatta Airport. The airline flies to more African destination than any other airline. It also connects Africa to India, Europe, and North Korea among other global destinations.

7. British American Tobacco Kenya Limited

British America Tobacco is popularly known as BAT is also among the biggest companies in the country. Their main operation involves manufacturing of cigarettes for domestic and export consumption. Its operations in Kenya began in 1907 and since then, it has grown to become the market leader of cigarettes manufacturing. The company is among the top employers in the country.

8. Standard Chartered Kenya

The bank is also among the financial institutions in the country with licenses from the Central Bank of Kenya, the regulator of national banking. Stanchart Kenya is also a subsidiary of the renown British multinational financial conglomerate.

The company prides itself as theoldest in this great country, and is also one of the country’s primary providers of financial services. It’s first two branches were opened on January 1911. Currently, Stanchart has grown to have a huge network composed of 32 branches strategically locatedin different parts of the country.

9. Bamburi Cement

The cement manufacturer bases its operations in the limestone rich coastal city of Mombasa and their cement is sold in different countries of the continent. It was established in the country in 1951, but the company started running later on in 1954. The head office of the company is located in Nairobi, Kenya.

The company has offered employment to Kenyans with over 800 employees working in different sectors of production. Bamburi Cement manufactures quality products satisfying their customers and that is why it’s able to export its products to Sri Lanka, Comoros, Madagascar, Seychelles, and Congo, among other countries.

10. Barclays Bank

Barclays Bank is a provider of financial services in the market and has been in operation in Kenya for over 100 years. The bank has 120 outlets and 234 ATMs throughout the country. The impressive financial performance of the bank has build confidence among the bank’s shareholders.
