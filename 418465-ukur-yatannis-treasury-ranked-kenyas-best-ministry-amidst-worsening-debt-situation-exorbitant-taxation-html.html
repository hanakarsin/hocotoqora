Ukur Yatani's Treasury Ranked Kenya's Best Ministry Amidst Worsening Debt Situation, Exorbitant Taxa

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Thursday, April 18, 2024
  • The Ministry of Finance edged over the other 20 ministries in a performance report released by Public Service Performance Management and Monitoring Unit (PSPMMU)
  • The Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Fisheries was ranked the worst performing out of the 21 ministries
  • However, no ministry or state department performed over par, with the National Treasury only achieving the "good" score, far from the "excellent” or “very good”

The Ukur Yattani-led Ministry of Finance is the top-performing ministry in the Jubilee government, a report from the Public Service Performance Management and Monitoring Unit (PSPMMU) has shown.

Despite the state of the country's debt, the National Treasury edged over the other 20 ministries, PSPMMU saying the performance was not the best for the ministry, adding COVID-19 had an impact.

Nevertheless, the Treasury only made it to the "good" score, failing to meet the "excellent" or "very good" scores reserved for the over-par performance.

The ranking was based on selected parameters based on service delivery and the impacts thereafter.

These included absorption of allocated government funds, absorption of externally mobilised resources, core mandate, project completion rate, access to government procurement opportunities, youth internships, industrial attachments and apprenticeships, and corruption prevention.

Others in the top five positions were State House, Adan Mohamed-led Ministry of East African Community and Regional Development, Office of the Attorney General and Department of Justice (headed by Kihara Kariuki) and Sicily Kariuki's Ministry of Water, Sanitation and Irrigation.

The National Treasury feat succeeds public outcry on the country's debt status and exorbitant taxation following the signing into law the 2021 Financial Bill.

The Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Fisheries was ranked the worst-performing ministry.

The other bottom ministries according to the PSPMMU report were Joe Mucheru's Ministry of Information, Communication and Technology and Youth Affairs, the Ministry of Sports, Culture and Heritage headed by CS Amina Mohamed and the Najib Balala-led Ministry of Tourism and Wildlife.
