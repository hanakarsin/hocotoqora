Ex-MP Kabando wa Kabondo says William Ruto's arrogance cost him Uhuru's trust

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Friday, June 28, 2024

- Ex-lawmaker Kabando wa Kabondo observed Ruto and his loyalists were arrogant

- The former legislator said it was unlikely for Uhuru to support his deputy president

- DP's allies have been exchanging barbs with Uhuru's key lieutenants in the recent times

Former Mukuru-ini legislator Kabando wa Kabondo has observed sheer arrogance and bravado exhibited by Deputy President William Ruto is the reason he may not be the fifth president.

While responding to a sarcastic tweet by Ruto's loyalist Senator Kipchumba Murkomen, Kabando claimed the DP had lost President Uhuru Kenyatta's trust he enjoyed in 2013.

"My friend, sarcastic #TBT, tongue-in-cheek. Bill had a golden chance to succeed UK. All gone now, thanks to his weird bravado. All gone, thanks to URP arrogance. Is all gone, thanks to parallel ”Ikulu” Sugoi. All gone, thanks to Bill’s “genius“ 21-26/4/2017. Bro,cry not, disembark," he said.

Uhuru and Ruto, who were previously referred to simply as UhuRuto dynamic duo, mounted a campaign in 2013 and more often than not, the two leaders walked hand in hand to public functions.

During that period, they usually stepped out in red ties and white shirts, the two were working closely and cordially more like brothers than political allies.

However, in the recent past days, the two have been trading barbs in public forums particularly in relation to the fight against corruption where they have differed greatly.

On Tuesday, August 27, the DP was forced to respond to a local publication on an apparent fall out within the Jubilee Party.

"Jubilee is under one leader Uhuru Kenyatta. The 'wars' are a figment of the fertile evil imaginations of the now notoriously desperate Standard Media's warped and venomous logic," wrote the DP.

Ruto made the remarks through his Twitter handle on Tuesday, August 27, in response to an article by one of the local dailies on the selection of McDonald Mariga to vie for Kibra seat.
