Uhuru Kenyatta Won't Allow Ruto and Raila to Team Up in 2022, Herman Manyora

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Monday, June 17, 2024

- Manyora predicted that if Raila Odinga and William Ruto come together to form an alliance, their political impact will be negative, especially on Uhuru and his side

- The analyst stated that the duo would be a major political force to reckon with, which is why Uhuru may do all that is possible to frustrate their coalition plans if there is any

- Manyora also said that Uhuru still needed Raila to advance his (Uhuru's) agenda in and after 2022

- It had been believed that in the event Raila falls out with Uhuru, the former will have Ruto as his option for a political realignment

Political analyst Herman Manyora has observed that President Uhuru Kenyatta cannot stand a coalition designed by his deputy William Ruto and ODM leader Raila Odinga given the kind of energy the coalition can bear.

In his latest Youtube commentary, the University of Nairobi scholar opined that Uhuru and his team were not naive to allow such an arrangement going by how formidable the duo can become if they form a coalition.

"None of his (Uhuru's) guys can beat Ruto and Raila. You can imagine the energy the two will bring into the campaign when they chose to work together, who in the Musalia Mudavadi team can face Raila and Ruto combined...None!" Manyora stated.

This follows rumours that had come about regarding a possible 2022 alliance between Ruto and Raila after the presumed fall of the 2018 handshake.

Manyora predicted that if the two are allowed to work together, the aftermath will not be good for Uhuru and his side.

"Uhuru being the man heading and receiving the intelligence reports in this country, he is unlikely to betray Raila in that manner because the consequences will not be beautiful," Manyora said.

The political commentator stated that the whispers concerning the Uhuru-Raila handshake were being spun by the ODM brigade, who were 'overwhelmed' by excitement, thus misinterpreting Uhuru's motive regarding the handshake.

The reactions of ODM members, therefore, did not represent the position of Uhuru and Raila in relation to their truce. The brigade had resorted to calling for a Raila-Ruto formation.

Manyora also added that the silence by Uhuru regarding the state of the handshake did not mean that he was in to betray Raila.

"They (ODM) read everything in terms of 2022. When Uhuru is seen with other principals like Musalia Mudavadi, then they see it as Uhuru deserting Raila," the analyst said.

The ODM team, according to Manyora, had become uneasy after Uhuru seemed to have shifted his radar on other political players, thus appearing to have ditched Raila.

This comes after the emergence of the One Kenya Alliance, which is believed to have the president's blessings. The alliance brought together Musalia Mudavadi, Gideon Moi, Moses Wetangula and Kalonzo Musyoka.

The scholar said the president's motive with the One Kenya Alliance was to create a solid base upon which the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) will be passed and not grooming who to take over from him once he retires in 2022.

"It is not about 2022. I suspect what he (Uhuru) is doing is to build regional coalitions and blocs to lock Ruto out of the regions for purposes of BBI. Uhuru is strengthening the regional kingpins because of BBI and not the 2022 politics," he said.

Manyora held that even it were to be that Uhuru endorses any of the One Kenya Alliance principals, then Raila will still be instrumental in making the agenda succeed, thus quashing any chances of Raila being betrayed with regard to the handshake.

"If he intends to make one of them the president, say, Gideon Moi, he (Uhuru) will need to give it to a caretaker man like Raila Odinga, then he (Gideon) can become the president in 2027. Therefore the alliance is not about 2022 but BBI," Manyora said.
