West Pokot governor John Lonyangapuo has announced quitting the Kenya National African Union (KANU) party in whose ticket he was elected governor in 2017.

Lonyangapuo said the independence party had used the county assembly members to frustrate his leadership.

He said he would come up with another party which he will use to defend his seat in the next year's vote.

"Don't worry. If I did not lose the seat while vying on a dead party like KANU, how will I fail with my own?" posed Lonyangapuo while addressing a gathering in Makutano.

The professor of applied mathematics, as he fondly refers to himself, was the only governor elected on the KANU ticket.

Lonyangapuo's exit has dealt KANU a blow as its leader, Gideon Moi, prepares to give shots in national politics.

The first timer in the governors' council said KANU had flourished in West Pokot due to his efforts and thus him quitting will be a huge disservice to the party.

Even with the governor out, Senator Samuel Pogishio stated KANU still had its grip on the county.

The West Pokot county leadership has the majority of its members coming from KANU with 33 members of the county assembly having been elected on the party's ticket.

Lonyangapuo will be seeking to defend his seat in next year's general election.

He floored Simon Kachapin who had won the seat in 2013.

Last year, he asked United States President Donald Trump to facilitate the deportation of his deputy Nicholas Atudonyang over alleged insubordination.

He had claimed Atudonyang was part of a team fighting him.

The professor claimed the person he once trusted before had teamed up with Tiaty MP William Kamket, Kanu Secretary-General Nick Salat and Senator Samuel Poghisio to sabotage his county's functions.

"No wonder he ran away from West Pokot, now I know why he moved to Texas in the United States. Trump should in fact deport him as soon as possible. We have been thinking he has various challenges, we keep telling people he will come back, but he is actually killing the county," an angry Lonyangapuo said.

Lonyangapuo also accused his unseen deputy of blocking tenders awarded to other communities in favour of Pokots saying he was driving an ethnic agenda.
