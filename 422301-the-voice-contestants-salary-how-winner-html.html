All about The Voice television show

The Voice is a four-time Emmy Award-winning competition series that airs on NBC. The reality television competition franchise is a worldwide sensation.

It started with the singing competition The Voice Holland, created by Dutch television producer John de Mol. Today, the singing competition franchise is dominating the reality television space. As of 2021, the franchise is owned by ITV Studios.

The American version of the franchise is one of the most popular because of its unique and interesting format. It features five stages: the blind auditions, the battle rounds, the knockouts, the playoffs, and the live performance shows.

During the blind auditions, one of the most interesting parts, the coaches hear the contestants sing, but they face away from the stage. If the coach likes what they hear, they push a button that turns their chair and view the individuals singing. If more than one coach turns their seat, the participant gets to choose who they want to work with.

During battle rounds, coaches pit two team members against each other and sing the same song together. After the performance, the coaches choose the preferred contestants to advance to the next stage.

The knockout and playoffs are the most interesting as people get emotional. Whoever wins this stage advances to the live performance where 12 performers compete against each other.

Every week, the contestant with the lowest votes is sent home until only one wins. The winner goes home with the cash prize and a recording contract.

What is The Voice contestants salary?

Do contestants on The Voice get paid? It isn’t only the winner the enjoys something in return for taking part in the reality television show. The contestants are paid for their time as well, even if they do not win.

However, they are not paid the same way as the coaches and staff. The singers get a stipend, not a paycheck, and it is not clear how much compensation they receive.
