The 15 best online bartending schools to enroll in 2021

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Sunday, June 23, 2024

Bartending is a crucial profession in the hospitality industry. More and more drinking enthusiasts and bar owners are looking to boost their services by incorporating the latest mixology. There are various online bartending schools available for bartenders and individuals who have a passion for creating exciting drinks to entertain their guests. So, which are the best online bartending schools?

Online bartending schools teach a variety of skills. They delve into the history of different drinks, the various occasions when each can be served, and customer service for bar professionals.

The best online bartending schools in 2021

Online schools teaching bartending skills offer convenience to their clients who can't attend physical classes. Several professionals prefer this mode of learning because they can study as they continue with their regular jobs. Here are some of the online bartenders courses offered in various schools:

1. BarSmarts

BarSmarts is a self-acclaimed industry leader in the American online bartender education program and one of the best online bartending schools. The course was created by Pernod Ricard, the worldâ€™s second-largest wine and spirits distillery.

The 60-day-course was originally an in-person program that transitioned to an online learning platform. It is best suited for professionals and enthusiasts alike, and delves into the history of cocktails, how to serve them, service management and guided video tastings.

The class is taught by resident historian and correspondent for Esquire Magazine, David Wondrich. The course fee is $29 and is only available to participants who are 21 years and above.

2. Diageo Bar Academy

Diageo is another available option for netizens looking for a bartending school online. Initially, it was an initiative by the company to improve the quality of bar staff. The courses focus on high-skill techniques, service skills, product knowledge, and business operations offered by industry experts.

The bartending classes offered by Diageo are free of charge. One unique aspect of this course is the well-being and physical health modules, which are an excellent addition for those working in the stressful service world. The academy is also keen on keeping its members up-to-date with trends and news in the industry.

3. Jerry

Jerry is another bartender course online platform whose name is inspired by Jerry Thomas, also known as the godfather of cocktails. The program targets bar owners and food and beverage professionals who are taught the scope of preparation, beverage types, operations, ethics, and leadership.

Courses are taught by renowned bartenders like Monica Berg and James Goggin. Each lesson is 30 minutes long and starts from beginner progressing to advanced levels. Learners can choose to pay $10 per month to access all the courses or $30 per course.

4. A Bar Above Mixology

Chris Tunstall, the co-founder and owner of ''A Bar Above'' created it as an advanced cocktail program to bartenders. The mixology course is divided into three steps. The first one focuses on ingredients and products you can use and how bartenders can get the best out of each.

Step two teaches how to balance a cocktail on paper. The final step is a two-part multiple-choice and practical certification exam.

This course uses high definition (HD) videos and interactive quizzes and incorporates virtual community and mentorship opportunities. The course fee is $147, which comes with lifetime access to the assets, so learners can come back anytime to refresh their skills, learn new tricks from the community or network.

5. Hollow Leg

Hollow Leg is based in Chicago, and learners can choose to have in-person or virtual lessons. The bartender classes online program offered here focuses on the art and science of mixology and bartending.

The course also teaches interactive cocktail classes for public, private, and corporate events and hosts mixology parties, including making non-alcoholic cocktails, known as mocktails. The classes are ideal for group settings and not necessarily professionals.

The school charges a fee of between $35 and $45, and the classes are between 60 to 90 minutes. As the school refers to itself, Chicago's hidden gem seeks to leave its learners energized and ready to create.

6. MasterClass

Masterclass is the brainchild of two industry experts. Lynnette Marrero is a New-York based, award-winning bartender who teamed up with Ryan Chetiyawardana, a former titleholder of ''Tales of the Cocktails'' International Bartender of the Year. He also owns 'Dandelyan', a world-renowned bar.

The two take the students through the different topics and have incorporated practical cocktail making tips with some of their personal career experiences. Some of the lessons include stocking a home bar, creating various party cocktails, and curating a drink menu for any occasion.

Masterclass has 17 lessons, and participants pay a fee of $15 per month, which is billed annually. Students can also cancel their subscription at any time; another factor that makes it one of the best online bartending schools.

7. Ananas

The Ananas school offers a two-part program for bartender classes online, a learning platform, and a training program. Bar owners and managers prefer it because it teaches their hire skills, thus improving their hospitality prowess.

The first level covers mixology basics and all things wine, beer, cocktail, bar, and customer service. The cocktail class provides 52 cocktail recipes and how to mix them, while the customer service course covers hosting and how to prepare for guests.

The intermediate level is broken into targeted modules, which students can pick from based on the areas they wish to improve in. The courses offered at Ananas are all free of charge.

8. TIPS certification

TIPS (Training for Intervention Procedures) is an online bartending certification program that educates and champions responsible service, sale and consumption of alcohol. The course teaches the details of how to detect client behaviours that may indicate the beginning of intoxication to help you look out for them and other guests.

TIPS is a much-needed certification in the bartending business due to the frequent number of underage drinking and drunk driving cases, most of which cause fatal road accidents.

Successful completion of the course allows you to expedite your knowledge of being a responsible bartender and also serves as your bartending license. The course registration fee is $40.

9. Mix World-Class Cocktails

Mix World Class Cocktails is a product of Udemy. It puts together concepts of successful bartending and also provides tips on becoming a professional drink mixer. It features Paul Martin, who holds the world record for speed mixing and is an expert bartender.

The course covers topics like 5-star classic cocktail mixing and garnishments and comes at a fee of $29.99 with a 30-day money-back guarantee. This package includes 2.5 hours of on-demand video, two downloadable resources, full lifetime access, mobile and TV access and a certificate of completion.

10. Bartend Like a Mad Man

This mixology course is offered through Udemy by Katrina Rodzon, a professional bartender with over ten years of experience. She has a creative and unique approach to bartending and teaches from the comfort of her home. Katrina discusses the techniques involved in mixing four popular cocktails and shows her clients how to mix and match different liquors to create original drinks.

The charges for the course are $19.99, which caters for a 1-hour of on-demand video, seven articles, full lifetime access, mobile and TV access and a certificate of completion. The fee has a 30-day money-back guarantee.

11. European Bartender School

This self-acclaimed world's largest bartender school has an online learning platform known as ''The Essentials of Bartending.'' The course topics include making pro-looking and tasting cocktails, offering a foundation upon which clients can build a successful bartending career.

It also comprises 3.5 hours of on-demand videos, 34 bartending lessons, 27 cocktail recipes and an in-depth, downloadable study note. Students also get lifelong access so they can study whenever they like. Upon completion of the course, students will get a completion certificate. The course fee is $29.99.

12. ABC Bartending School

ABC Online School offers an online training program that learners can access on any computer or mobile device. Participants take the course at their own pace, and it has no time limit.

The school trains its students to work in any bar, restaurant, night club or cruise ship. They also learn various updated popular drinks, with a speciality in shots, beers, and wine. Students are also taught alcohol laws and responsible liquor service.

ABC also goes further to help its trainees with nationwide job placement. The course fee is $39.99, which is inclusive of all course material and tuition.

13. Bartending 101

This is another of Udemy online bartending courses hosted by Michael Rego. Bartending 101 teaches students how to create seven of the best drinks available in the market with style and flair.

Michael uses HD videos to elaborate on what he is doing clearly. The course is free of charge and teaches learners how to blend and shake drinks like a pro and creates unique and creative drink blends enjoyed by guests.

14. Bartending Mastery

Bartending Mastery falls under the Udemy paid bartending courses and was created by Michael Miller, a world-renowned licensed bartender. He focuses on customer service as a crucial aspect of the bartending industry, focusing on presentation, social adaptability and ordering knowledge.

15. Harvard Bartending Course

The popular business and law school also boasts an online bartending services course, which has been in existence since 1964. The program is hosted by Ben Stoller, who has taught bartenders for over two decades.

Ben believes that the experience should be fun and exciting and shows his students how to create any mixture by only knowing the colour and taste of a drink. The course fee is a whopping $49.99. Students who complete the course attain a Harvard Bartending Course certificate of completion.
