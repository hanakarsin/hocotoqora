Homa Bay: Section of County Employees Pocket 3 Times Their Salary after Payment Error

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Saturday, July 13, 2024
  • The payment error left close to 3,000 other employees without salaries and would have to wait until the end of the month
  • HR director Omondi Auma linked the salary hitch to an error caused by operational issues at Cooperative and Absa banks
  • Auma apologised for the error, saying the county was working to resolve the issue

A section of Homa Bay county government employees was pleasantly surprised after pocketing up to three times their salary following a payment error.

However, as some workers were awash with the cash, the error left close to 3,000 other employees without salaries and would have to wait until the end of the month.

The county human resource management director Omondi Auma linked the salary hitch to an error caused by operational issues at Cooperative and Absa banks in which the employees are paid.

“The error was caused by operational issues at the banks while they were processing the salary payments for June. The county government is working with the banks to address the issue,” said Auma as reported by Nation.

Auma apologised for the error saying the county was working to resolve the issue and have those who were not paid receive their June salaries.

Return the money

The celebrations of those who have been overpaid were cut short after the county asked them to return the extra money.

In a memo by the county human resource department, those who would not surrender the overpayment would be deducted from their salaries of the succeeding months untill all the money is recovered.

Worker loses money to State

Elsewhere, an official serving in the Mandera county government lost KSh 62 million to the State after failing to account for it.
