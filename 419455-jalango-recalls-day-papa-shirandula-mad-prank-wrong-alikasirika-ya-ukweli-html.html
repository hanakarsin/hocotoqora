Comedian turned radio presenter Jalang'o has revealed that Charles Bukeko, aka Papa Shirandula, was a no-nonsense man who sometimes had no sense of humour.

Speaking at Papa Shirandula's memorial on Sunday, May 18, Jalang'o recalled the day he and Otoyo got themselves in hot soup after pranking the veteran actor.

"I remember the episode that I enjoyed shooting was when Otoyo and I went to buy Papa a brand new Hummer. We didn't know which job he was doing because, on the show, he portrayed himself as a rich in-law," Jalang'o said.

They went to a car yard and chose a Hummer for him.

"Hii gari ndio inakufaa. Utachukua kifunguo halafu utalipia pale (This car suits your class. Take the keys and pay for it)," the duo said.

But things didn't go well.

"Ujue Papa alikasirika ya ukweli (Papa got so angry he didn't know it was acting). We had amazing memories with him, but we definitely didn't have a chance to say goodbye because everything happened quickly," Jalang'o added.

The Kiss FM presenter revealed that the name Jalang'o he was given by Papa same as Otoyo.

"Papa opened for us doors we could only dream of. Alitupatia jembe nendeni mkalime hakutupea mazao (He didn't give us fish, he taught us how to fish)," Otoyo said.

Jalang'o and Otoyo played stubborn in-laws of Papa Shirandula on the show and would visit his home unannounced to see their sister Wilbroda.

Jalang'o, Otoyo and Njoro said they were grateful to Shirandula for giving them a platform to sharpen their skills and promised to reciprocate by educating his daughter up to university.

Great man

Wilbroda broke down in tears remembering her on-screen husband.

Speaking in an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke, during Papa Shirandula's first death anniversary, Wilbroda said:

"He was an amazing actor and a perfectionist on set."

She further revealed that Papa died after shooting five episodes on his new show dubbed Mzee ni Wewe, which was yet to air soon.

"Papa used to push us to be great. Things have been slow since he passed away and we're taking it a step at a time," she explained.
