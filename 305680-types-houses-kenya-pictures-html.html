Types of houses in Kenya (with pictures)

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Thursday, August 8, 2024

In the last few years, Kenya has experienced a resurgence in real estate development. Of particular interest are the numerous residential units that have dotted the country that continue to increase each day significantly. Numerous factors have contributed to the current development, but we can keep the topic for another day. At the moment, it will be appropriate to focus on the different types of houses in Kenya.

It is very easy to loosely term houses in Kenya as this or that even without paying much attention to the style, scale, or even the spatial arrangement. The parameters should ideally determine what type a house is. In different parts of the world, people have adopted style as the defining factor on the various house categories. This is the greatest determinant in defining the following types of houses with pictures and definitions.

What are the different types of houses in Kenya?

As you look at the different variations of houses, it is essential to bear in mind that most Kenyan architecture in the towns and cities borrow their inspiration from European countries. That is why the terminologies used and styles mentioned will seem to reflect those of the region. Just like in Europe, brick and mortar are Kenya’s most popular building components. While it may be true that this list may not be as exhaustive with several points of departure as a result of the parameters used, this content represents the general scenario on the ground. So what are different kinds of houses?

Contemporary homes

This specifically refers to a wide range of houses that were designed by architects and built in recent decades. The homes in this category place their emphasis on simple designs and geometric lines. Contemporary homes succeeded the international style, and this reflects just how dynamic and experimental the post-colonialism generation of builders was. This resulted in a period that saw the integration of great modernist ideas from Europe and western countries.

Generally, contemporary homes are those you would find in the countryside. Just like most of our grandfathers’ houses, the house design features plenty of open floor plans, lots of glass windows, and incredibly inventive designs. Also, the exteriors of homes in the contemporary category come from dynamic textures and materials. This is aimed at avoiding unnecessary detail as well as elaborate ornamentation.

Mansionette homes

A mansion goes beyond being just a residence – it is also a symbol. Size may not be significant, and the answer depends on who you ask. There are realtors who may perceive houses measuring 5,000 square feet to qualify as a mansion, while others believe that a mansion must measure at least 8,000 square feet. In affluent sections of the different cities in the country, only homes measuring over 20,000 feet can qualify.

Different members of the political class are known to own these majestic homes. For instance, the former prime minister Raila Odinga has one beautiful home located near the Kisumu International Airport. The house has ten bedrooms, a humongous helipad, jacuzzis, a theatre, state of the art kitchens, and conference halls.

Mabati houses

Most may have the perception that when a person lives in a fabricated house, then it means that there are struggling financially. However, mabati house ideas can create incredible permanent homes. A well-built mabati house will leave your neighbors and friends wishing to include the idea in their plans. Before beginning any construction work, it is ideal that you have an estimate of the cash that would be sufficient for the entire project.

Apartment houses in Kenya

By definition, an apartment may be a single unit in one big building that a family or person can use as a dwelling place. An apartment can be occupied by either a tenant or owner. In this country, the word ‘flat’ is always used as a synonym of the same, but other markets consider it to be one of lesser quality. That is why in Kenya, you will never hear that someone resides in an apartment in Eastlands or a flat in Lavington.

The growth of these housing units has been rapid yet steady, especially in upmarket areas such as Kileleshwa, Lavington, Kilimani, etc. where mansions have been removed to give way for this development. The houses may range from one up to four bedrooms, and some come with servants’ quarters. Developers add salient features such as swimming pools, clubhouses, ponds, and much more. Their appeal lies in their amenities, size, style, and number of units, but the price is usually the real determinant. Mostly, these residences are occupied by upper-middle-income earners. With the recent upsurge in the number of these housing units, the number of middle-income earners has grown significantly.

Earth houses

What is more friendly to the earth itself than an earth house? Houses created from the soil beneath our feet are the ideal example of what sustainable construction should look like. Interestingly, the longest standing buildings on the planet are earthen. So next time you see those mud-walled hoses in the countryside, just know someone out there is doing the environment a favor.

Condominiums (condo) in Kenya

Used as a short form of the word Condominium, these are living spaces quite similar to apartments. The only difference is that these can be sold independently and therefore are treated as real estate. One great example of such a building is the Eighty Eight Nairobi Condominium that is currently under construction in Upper Hill. The capital city is also home to several other similar residences such as the Heri Heights Serviced Apartments, Exeter Rose Apartments, The Ridge by Cytonn Real Estate, Nairobi Gamepark Apartments, and Chalbi Condominiums.

Town House

This term was used in the pre-World War I era to refer to a huge house owned by aristocrats and located in the city. But here in Kenya, we use this term to refer to something slightly different. Generally, you will find the houses mostly in the upmarket areas – particularly in gated compounds. There may be two or three levels, including a plinth area measuring approximately 250-400 square meters.

Just like apartments, the common areas and facilities here are shared, but they offer more operational codes and exclusive features that make the communities seem like clubs with private members. These residences boast salient features like clubhouses, swimming pools, basketball courts, tennis courts, boreholes, gyms, landscaped gardens, etc. Notably, these residences are usually under the protection of private security firms as well as the Kenya police.

Container homes

These type of houses are majorly created from shipping containers. Thanks to creative Kenyan designers, there are different ways that you can transform this transportation asset and turn it into a comfortable living space at a significantly lower budget. It will take a little addition to the interior or exterior, and you will have yourself a functional home that can have as much as three bedrooms.

Not only is this type of house secure, but it is also easy to set up and affordable as well. Also, it is among the few house varieties that can be moved from one location to the other without much hassle. British/European rustic designs

This architectural design has deep roots in the pre-colonial era and was popularized by the British settlers who brought along with them their sensibilities accompanied by their home design ideas.

Another great example is Njoro’s Lord Egerton Castle, which stands at a driving distance of 30 minutes from Nakuru town. With its British-inspired design, the fascinating architecture stands within canopy trees, lush lawns, and numerous shrubs. It was built by Lord Egerton himself from 1938 to 1952.

African architecture

The country has not only relied on foreign inspiration to come up with the vast architectural creations, and there are many modern house designs that still keep the African heritage. This is most evident in the roofing used, flooring, and shapes of houses. From the mud and grass thatched houses of western Kenya to the limestone and makuti houses of the Coast, it is evident that culture cannot be easily eroded from the minds of Kenyans.

One of the most popular modern designs that borrow some African inspiration is the Tiwi Beach Hotel in Mombasa.
