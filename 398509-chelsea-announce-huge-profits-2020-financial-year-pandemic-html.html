Chelsea announce huge profits in 2020 financial year despite pandemic

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Tuesday, April 9, 2024

The Blues made a string of signings in the summer for a combined £200million

Despite this, the club announced they had made profits in the last financial year

Qualification for the UEFA Champions League largely helped Chelsea’s cause in their financial front

Chelsea football club have announced that they made an impressive £32.5million (KSh 4.8 billion) profit in their latest financial results despite taking a massive financial hit due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

The Blues splashed out more than £200million in the summer transfer window to acquire several superstars despite the fact that other clubs were kept their budget on the lower side because of the financial implications of the forced football break between March and June.

Lampard acquired the likes of Timo Werner, Kai Havertz, Ben Chilwell, Edouard Mendy, Thiago Silva and Hakim Ziyech as they sought to get back to their title winning ways this term.

The Mirror UK reports that much of that spending occurred after the cut off of the latest financial results, which was quoted at £93.7m in the financial year ending June 2020.

At the time, the Blues already had deals on the table for several of their new signings.

Since then, however, Chelsea have still been impressive in their financial figures if the fact that their turnover was reduced from £446.7m to £407.4m due to the pandemic.

The fact that they finished fourth and in Champions League places last season also did a lot to help the club’s financial cause.

While they spent a lot in the transfer market, the Blues also cashed in from transfers, particularly with the sale of Eden Hazard to Real Madrid for a reported £130m.

Other superstars including Alvaro Morata, Mario Palasic, David Luiz, Ola Aina, Tomas Kalas, Michael Hector and Kenneth Omeruo were also offloaded on permanent transfers, which eased the club’s payroll while also streaming in some funds in transfer fees.

Chelsea chairman Bruce Buck spoke on the club’s finances as he broke down what led to their profits.

“In common with many, many businesses across the globe, the pandemic has had a significant impact on Chelsea’s income but it is a sign of the strength and stability of our financial operation that the company was still able to post a profit in the past financial year,” he said as quoted by The Sun."This was done while continuing to invest in our playing staff and indeed had normal football not halted in March, projections show a record profit and record turnover would have been achieved,”"That would have represented an increase in revenue for a fifth year in succession,”

Chelsea on a slippery slope in the EPL

Despite thriving on the financial front, the Blues are in a worrying rut in the English Premier League as their season took a dramatic turn in December 2020.
