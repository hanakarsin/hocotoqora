Doreen Moracha Says Babu wa Loliondo Made Her Believe She Was HIV Free for 2 years

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Monday, January 1, 0001
  • Doreen Moracha was born with HIV and growing up with the condition, she was stigmatised most of her life, which led to her not pursuing her career
  • Desperate to turn her life around after clearing her secondary education, Moracha turned to herbal treatment in the hope that she would be cured of the disease
  • She joined the masses that had flocked to Loliondo to drink a herbal medicine that was being offered by the traditional healer
  • It was after this trip that she stopped taking her ARV medication in the belief that she had been healed

Ambilikile Mwasapila aka Babu wa Loliondo, a retired Lutheran rural pastor from Tanzania, became East Africa's most recognisable television personality and healer in 2011.

He'd had dreams in which God revealed the recipe for herbal medicine that could cure any ailment.

The old pastor's service was enthusiastically praised by Lutheran bishops who had all but abandoned him in his prior rural mission field. However, the response among Charismatic churches was mixed.

The government backed him up while the national research hospital gave a hazy endorsement to the medicine, which is the same as traditional medicine among various ethnic groups.

Thus, in this ministry, modern scientific, Christian, and traditional worldviews suddenly corresponded, easing the tensions between the three lifeworld of Tanzanian Christians.

But after the deaths of several HIV-positive patients who had abandoned antiretroviral (ARV) drugs, the magic of the healer vanished.

Moracha visiting Loliondo

Among the many HIV patients who visited the magic healer for a cup of his healing concoction was AYP Advocacy AWARD Winner Doreen Moracha.

In a brief Facebook post after Babu Lowaliondo's death, Moracha revealed she believed she was cured of HIV after visiting him and even stopped taking her ARV medication for two years.

"Babu wa Loliondo made me believe I was HIV free for 2 years. I stopped "drinking" ARVs anyhow. Earth is hard," she posted on Facebook.

Further narrating her experience and reasons why she decided to visit the healer, Moracha said she had been stigmatised for long,, and because of her condition, she could not pursue her career.

The young, brave lady felt overwhelmed and desperate to change her situation; she decided to visit Babu wa Loliondo when he had just started making headlines in media.

"When I faced all of this, I felt like I had to do something, I had to change my situation. Sometimes the universe hears us and yes the universe heard me and I saw Babu wa Loliondo on TV," she said.

She was able to convince her mother and together, they drove to Lolindo and had their share of the concoction and believing she had been healed, she immediatel stopped her ARV drugs.

She later contracted pneumonia as a result and was forced to go back to taking the drugs because the pain was to much to bear.

"The first time the viral load test was done after I defaulted the drugs was 200, 000 copies and the normal viral load is supposed to be 500 copies," she narrated. "...and with a straight face I told the doctor that I was taking my medication," she added.
